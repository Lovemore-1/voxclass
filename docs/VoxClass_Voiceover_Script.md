# VoxClass — Demo Video Voiceover Script
**Total runtime: ~3.5 min | Speak clearly, natural pace**

---

## [0:00 – 0:10] HOOK (title card visible)

> "Every lecture, students get lost — and they don't say anything.
> A raised hand takes courage. A red button doesn't.
> This is VoxClass."

---

## [0:10 – 0:22] SIGN IN (both screens visible)

> "VoxClass has two sides — the lecturer and the student.
> I'm signing into the lecturer account on the left,
> and the student account on the right, simultaneously."

---

## [0:22 – 0:36] SESSION CREATED + STUDENT JOINS (QR code visible)

> "Once I'm in, I create a session called 'Struct of Data'.
> VoxClass generates a unique 6-digit code — STG3MH.
> On the right, the student enters that code and their name
> to join instantly. No accounts required for students."

---

## [0:36 – 0:52] UPLOAD PPTX (file picker visible)

> "Now I upload my actual lecture slides — a 32-slide PPTX
> from an APU Data Structures module.
> VoxClass handles PDFs, PowerPoints, and images."

---

## [0:52 – 1:05] LIVE PRESENTATION STARTS (both screens showing slide)

> "I hit Present, and both screens update at the same time.
> As I navigate slides, the student's view follows mine automatically —
> they're always on the same page, literally.
> This is powered by Supabase Realtime WebSockets."

---

## [1:05 – 1:30] STUDENT REACTS (student taps Confused)

> "The student is viewing slide 9 — 'What is Data Structures'.
> They're not following, so they tap Confused.
> Watch the lecturer screen on the left."

*(pause 2 seconds)*

> "Instantly — one red dot. No delay.
> The reaction count updates in real-time."

---

## [1:30 – 1:50] CONFUSION ALERT FIRES (red banner on lecturer screen)

> "When confusion crosses a threshold, a red alert appears:
> '1 student confused — 33% — consider pausing to clarify.'
> The lecturer never has to guess. The class tells them."

---

## [1:50 – 2:15] SIGNALS TAB — AI TOOLS

> "I switch to the Signals tab.
> This is where the AI tools live.
> I can tap 'Generate Clarifying Questions' and Gemini will analyse
> the current slide and generate targeted questions to re-engage the class.
> Or I can hit 'Re-explain This Topic' and get three different
> explanations — an analogy, step-by-step, and a concrete example —
> and push the best one to every student's screen."

---

## [2:15 – 2:48] QUESTIONS PUSHED TO STUDENT

> "The questions are pushed live.
> On the student's screen, they appear as cards from the lecturer.
> 'What questions do you still have about this topic?'
> 'How would you apply this material in a real-world scenario?'
> The student can type an answer, or tap 'See AI Answer'
> to get an explanation from Gemini."

---

## [2:48 – 3:07] ANONYMOUS QUESTIONS

> "Students can also ask questions anonymously — no names, no fear.
> Those appear in the lecturer's Signals tab in real-time.
> Gemini can cluster them into themes so the lecturer knows
> what the whole class is confused about, not just one person."

---

## [3:07 – 3:27] WRAP UP

> "VoxClass was built in one week for the Shortcut Asia Internship Challenge.
> Flutter, Supabase Realtime, and Gemini 1.5 Flash.
> Every feature you saw — live sync, reactions, AI questions —
> was built from scratch.
> The problem is real. The solution is live.
> And the code is on GitHub."

---

## DELIVERY TIPS
- Speak at a natural pace — don't rush to fill silence
- The pauses between sections are intentional — let the screen do the talking
- When you say "Watch the lecturer screen" — pause and let the reaction happen visually
- Record in a quiet room; you can use your phone or a laptop mic
- Upload to YouTube (unlisted) and paste the link in the submission form
